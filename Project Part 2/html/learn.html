Multiple Myeloma

Marty was diagnosed with Multiple Myeloma which is a form of cancer formed by malignant plasma cells. Multiple myeloma causes cancer cells to accumulate in the bone marrow, where they crowd out healthy blood cells. Rather than produce helpful antibodies, the cancer cells produce abnormal proteins that can cause kidney problems.

What multiple myeloma does to a persons bones

Upon being diagnosed with multiple myeloma, Marty started chemo at a hospital in Arizona. As the days progressed Marty started to show more and more signs of improvement and was even able to go home. In the following weeks, Marty's blood counts continued to rise and he even started to gain back his weight and play catch with my little cousin Eric. Everything seemed to be going well until July when his cancer came back and the fight continued.

The relapse hit him really hard in early July. He was hospitalized twice and started plasmaphersis treatments and then a chemo regimen. Unfortunately, Multiple Myeloma is very brutal and Marty was forced to give it everthing he had. His treatment would be very hard and risky, but despite this Marty was strong, optimistic and determined not to give up. On September 1st 2015 my uncle Marty lost his battle with cancer and heaven gained a new angel. Rest In Peace Marty.
