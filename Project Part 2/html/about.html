About Uncle Marty

Growing up

My Aunt and Uncle only lived close to me for a short period of time while I was in elementary school. One thing I remember discretely from this time was going over their house while my parents went out.

For dinner my Aunt Tara made mac and cheese and brussel sprouts. I scarfed down the mac and cheese but once I got to the brussel sprouts they were not appealing to me. I refused to eat them and this is when I learned that Uncle Marty just like Aunt Tara would not concede to my stubbornness. I sat their all night looking at those brussel sprouts until I eventually admitted defeat and ate those tasteless vegetables.

Visting Marty and family in Arizona

Living in Northeastern Pennsylvania made it very difficult to visit my family in Arizona but my family and I tried to make it a habit of visting them during summer break. One summer, when I was still in Middle School, we came down and we just spent our time relaxing at their beautiful house. During this time Uncle Marty enjoyed playing around with us and having a Good time. I remember him pushing us into the pool and throwing a nerf football into the pool so we could make a game winning catch into the water. My Mom who liked to lay by the pool also got a nice surprise when Uncle Marty turned on the sprinklers while she was tanning.

Lessons Learned

Even though I was only able to visit them seldomly in Arizona, I was able to learn many important lessons from him when I was there. He taught me that you get what you deserve when he tapped my brother and cousin together for tapping our younger cousin to a chair. He showed me that family and friends are very valuable by letting me play with his buddies in poker night even though I wasn't very good. Lastly he taught me that you can disagree with a lot of people and they can say things about you behind your back, but their view doesn't describe you. It doesn't define you as a friend, a father, a uncle, a husband, or even a son.
